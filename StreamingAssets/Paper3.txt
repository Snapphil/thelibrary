ABSTRACT

An introduction to quantum computing principles, from the fundamental qubit to quantum algorithms. We explore superposition, entanglement, quantum gates, and the revolutionary potential of quantum computers to solve problems intractable for classical systems.

1. THE QUBIT

Classical Bit: Either 0 OR 1

Quantum Bit (Qubit): 0 AND 1 simultaneously!
  |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
  where |α|² + |β|² = 1

Key Properties:
• Superposition: Exists in multiple states at once
• Measurement: Collapses to definite state (0 or 1)
• Bloch Sphere: 3D representation of qubit state

2. QUANTUM ENTANGLEMENT

When qubits become entangled, measuring one instantly determines the other - regardless of distance!

Bell State: |Φ+⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2

Properties:
• Non-local correlations
• Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance"
• Foundation for quantum cryptography
• Enables quantum teleportation

3. QUANTUM GATES

Hadamard (H):
  Creates superposition
  H|0⟩ = (|0⟩ + |1⟩)/√2

Pauli-X (NOT):
  Flips the qubit
  X|0⟩ = |1⟩

Pauli-Z:
  Phase flip
  Z|1⟩ = -|1⟩

CNOT (Controlled-NOT):
  Two-qubit gate
  Creates entanglement

Phase Gates (S, T):
  Rotate phase of |1⟩ state

4. QUANTUM ALGORITHMS

Shor's Algorithm (Factoring):
• Factors large numbers exponentially faster
• Complexity: O((log N)³) vs O(e^N) classical
• Threatens RSA encryption!

Grover's Algorithm (Search):
• Searches unsorted database
• Quadratic speedup: O(√N) vs O(N)
• Applications: optimization, databases

VQE (Variational Quantum Eigensolver):
• Chemistry simulations
• Drug discovery applications
• Materials science

5. QUANTUM ADVANTAGE

Task: Factor 2048-bit RSA number
• Classical computer: ~300 trillion years
• Quantum computer: ~10 seconds

Task: Search N items
• Classical: O(N) operations
• Quantum: O(√N) operations

Task: Simulate molecules
• Classical: Exponential scaling
• Quantum: Polynomial scaling

6. CURRENT STATE OF THE ART

Leading Systems:
• IBM Condor: 1,121 qubits
• Google Sycamore: 72 qubits
• IonQ Forte: 32 qubits (trapped ion)
• Quantinuum H2: 32 qubits (highest fidelity)

Challenges:
• Decoherence (quantum states decay)
• Error correction overhead
• Extreme cooling requirements
• Scalability
