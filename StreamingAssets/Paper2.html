Environmental Research Visualization

Global Climate Change:
A Data-Driven Analysis of Temperature Anomalies

Dr. Emma Rodriguez, Prof. James Liu, Dr. Fatima Hassan
Climate Research Institute, MIT & NOAA

Abstract

This interactive visualization presents comprehensive climate data spanning 1880-2024, highlighting the accelerating trend of global temperature anomalies. Through dynamic charts and regional impact assessments, we demonstrate the urgent need for climate action. Our analysis reveals that the past decade has recorded the highest temperatures in modern history, with significant implications for ecosystems, agriculture, and human populations worldwide.

7. Conclusion

The data unequivocally shows that climate change is accelerating. Without immediate and sustained action, we face irreversible damage to ecosystems and human societies. The visualizations in this paper demonstrate both the severity of the crisis and the potential for mitigation through coordinated global action.
