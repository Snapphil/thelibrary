CLIMATE CHANGE DATA ANALYSIS
Global Temperature & Environmental Trends

Dr. Maria Santos, Prof. James Liu, Dr. Emma Björk
MIT Climate Research Center

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ABSTRACT

Comprehensive analysis of global temperature anomalies, CO₂ levels, sea level rise, and climate projections based on NASA, NOAA, and international research data spanning 1850-2024.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

KEY STATISTICS

• Global Warming: +1.2°C since 1850
• CO₂ Level: 424 ppm (highest in 800,000 years)
• Sea Level Rise: +21 cm since 1900
• Arctic Ice Loss: -13% per decade

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. GLOBAL TEMPERATURE TRENDS

Historical Temperature Anomalies:
• 1850-1900: Baseline (0°C reference)
• 1980s: +0.18°C average
• 1990s: +0.32°C average
• 2000s: +0.51°C average
• 2010s: +0.82°C average
• 2020s: +1.15°C average (ongoing)

Recent Records:
• 2023: Hottest year on record (+1.18°C)
• 2024: On track to exceed 2023 (+1.29°C)
• July 2024: Hottest month ever recorded

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2. ATMOSPHERIC CO₂

Current Level: 424 ppm

Historical Comparison:
• Pre-industrial (1750): 280 ppm
• 1960 (Keeling Curve start): 317 ppm
• 1990: 354 ppm
• 2000: 370 ppm
• 2010: 390 ppm
• 2024: 424 ppm

Rate of Increase: 2.5 ppm/year
(Fastest rate in 800,000 years)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3. SEA LEVEL RISE

Current Rate: 3.7 mm/year (accelerating)
Total Rise Since 1900: +21 cm

Contributing Factors:
• Thermal expansion: 42%
• Glacier melt: 21%
• Greenland ice sheet: 15%
• Antarctic ice sheet: 8%
• Water storage changes: 14%

Projections for 2100:
• Low emissions scenario: +30-60 cm
• High emissions scenario: +60-110 cm

4. REGIONAL IMPACTS

Temperature Changes by Region:
• Arctic: +3.8°C (2x global average)
• Northern Europe: +2.1°C
• North America: +1.5°C
• Central Europe: +1.7°C
• Mediterranean: +1.5°C
• Tropics: +0.9°C
• Antarctica: +1.8°C

5. EXTREME WEATHER EVENTS

Frequency Increase Since 1980:
• Heat waves: +300%
• Severe droughts: +29%
• Category 4-5 hurricanes: +25%
• Flooding events: +134%
• Wildfires: +78%

Economic Impact (2023):
• Climate disasters: $380 billion
• Lives affected: 185 million people

7. MITIGATION STRATEGIES

• Renewable energy transition (solar, wind)
• Carbon capture and storage technology
• Reforestation and ecosystem restoration
• Electric vehicle adoption
• Energy efficiency standards
• Methane emission reduction
• Sustainable agriculture practices

CONCLUSION

The data unequivocally demonstrates accelerating climate change driven by human activities. Without immediate and substantial reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, global warming will exceed the 1.5°C threshold by 2030, leading to irreversible impacts on ecosystems and human societies.
